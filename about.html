A Legacy of Excellence

Our History

1996

Groundbreaking

Construction begins on a bold new home for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, funded through a Hillsborough County sales tax measure passed by Tampa Bay voters.

1998

Grand Opening

Houlihan's Stadium (later renamed) opens its doors. The Buccaneers play their first home game in the new facility to a sold-out crowd of 65,647.

2001

Super Bowl XXXV

Tampa hosts the first of three Super Bowls at Raymond James, cementing its status as one of the NFL's premier event destinations.

2002

Naming Rights & Renovations

Raymond James Financial acquires naming rights. The iconic full-scale Pirate Ship is installed in the north end zone, becoming the stadium's most recognizable landmark.

2009

Super Bowl XLIII

The stadium hosts its second Super Bowl, with the Pittsburgh Steelers defeating the Arizona Cardinals 27–23 in one of the most thrilling championship games in NFL history.

2016–2018

$160M Renovation

A major renovation transforms the venue: dual 6,000 sq ft 4K LED scoreboards installed, a new sound system, expanded concourse, and upgraded club-level amenities.

2021

Super Bowl LV & Championship Glory

The Tampa Bay Buccaneers win Super Bowl LV on their home field — the first team in NFL history to win a Super Bowl in their home stadium. Over 25,000 fans (and many vaccinated healthcare workers) witness history.

2025

The Future Begins

Raymond James Stadium launches an expanded events calendar, new premium hospitality packages, and partnerships with the world's top touring artists and promoters.

Directions & Parking

Getting to RayJay

Raymond James Stadium is located at 4201 N Dale Mabry Hwy, Tampa, FL 33607 — just minutes from downtown Tampa and Tampa International Airport.

By Car

  • From I-275 N: Take Exit 41A (Dale Mabry Hwy N), drive north 1.5 miles
  • From I-275 S: Take Exit 42 (Himes Ave), turn right on Martin Luther King Jr. Blvd
  • From I-4 W: Merge onto I-275 S toward Tampa, follow above directions

By Transit

  • HART Bus Routes 12, 34, and 39 stop adjacent to the stadium
  • Ride-share drop-off: Dale Mabry Hwy at Spruce St (Gate D)
  • Bicycle parking available at all gates — bring your lock

Common Questions

FAQ

What is the clear bag policy? +
Raymond James Stadium follows the NFL Clear Bag Policy. Fans may carry one clear bag (no larger than 12" × 6" × 12") plus one small clutch bag (no larger than 4.5" × 6.5"). Medical and diaper bags are permitted with additional screening.
Can I bring outside food or beverages? +
Outside food is generally not permitted. One factory-sealed water bottle (20 oz or less) may be brought in. Infants may bring formula, baby food, and juice boxes. All items are subject to security screening.
How early should I arrive before an event? +
We recommend arriving 90–120 minutes before kickoff or showtime. Gates typically open 2 hours prior to NFL games and 90 minutes before concerts. Check your specific event for gate opening times.
How do I book a private event or corporate function? +
Fill out our Event Inquiry Form and our events team will respond within 2 business days. For urgent inquiries, call us at (813) 870-3700. We host everything from 50-person board meetings to 10,000-person galas.
Is the stadium wheelchair accessible? +
Yes. Raymond James Stadium is fully ADA compliant. We offer companion seating, elevator access to all levels, accessible restrooms on every concourse, and a dedicated drop-off/pick-up zone at Gate D on the north side of the stadium.
